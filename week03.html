# Week 3 - Sharing collective progress report. - Preparing production process. - Pre­production: finalizing pre-production. - Team discussion. - Making: finalizing pre­production process. - Assignment: reading + your collective process report. ___________________________________ # Finalizing pre-production ## Collective process report presentation. Each team gives a process report presentation for sharing ideas and feedback in class. ## What to consider in planning Reference: Gahan, Andrew, 3ds Max Modeling for Games: Volume II. Focal Press, 2012. ### Schedule and resources - Ensure that you have enough time and resources to deliver it on time. Always ensure you know the deadline. ### Computational cost based on your delivery forms - Determine the polygon count (triangles or quadrangles), texture resolution specifications, and what types of textures are required (Bump, Specular, Normal maps, etc). ### High quality pre-production preparation - Visual design / concept art If you don’t have clear and detailed concepts for your project, don’t start modeling. It’s a lot easier to make changes in 2D and on sketches, than it is halfway through your 3D build. A lot of artists fail to do this – don’t fall into the same trap. It’s a worthwhile investment of time to make all your decisions up front. ## 3D modeling ### Installation Maya - Check the [week 1](https://jiharu.github.io/lowpoly/week01.html) Maya installation section to find a link to download the educational version of Maya. ### Action item If you haven't used Maya yet, please do the tutorial below.
