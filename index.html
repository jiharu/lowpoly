# DIGF-2012-001 Low Poly Game Art Winter 2018 **Course name:** Low Poly Game Art **Meetings:** Friday, 3:10pm–6:10pm **Location:** Room 425, Annex Building (MCC) **Course webpage:** Canvas & [http://jiharu.github.io/lowpoly/](http://jiharu.github.io/lowpoly/) **Course schedule:** [Google link](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1Ob78eq8feKOhmdlHK3AJx71cI1p53jYFvdmVWf0fhV0/edit#gid=984911569) **Office hours:** Thursday 2:30 to 3:30pm at 701G, 205 Richmond (hji at faculty.ocadu.ca by appointment) ## Course description Low polygon 3D art occur in real-time applications like digital games that have historically been constrained by the technologies used. However this approach to 3D modelling has become a contemporary art form evidenced across a wide range of contexts from art galleries to game consoles. This studio course surveys a broad range of approaches to building low poly game art exploring 3D production processes and contexts of delivery. Students will produce low poly playable experiences ready for exhibition. ## Supplies Students will be required to supply laptop, three button mouse, and headphones (to follow web tutorials) in the class. Please also bring a notebook or small sketchbook and pencils to sketch and collect ideas. ## Teaching & Learning Methods We will use a variety of learning strategies in this class including project work, discussions, presentations, tutorials, and lectures. ## Late Work All late work will be assessed a minimum penalty of 1 % of the work grade per week. Please remember that even with prior permission, no late work will be accepted more than 21 days after the initial due date. ## Participation Participation will include the following: - arriving on time and listening to lectures and instruction. - being prepared and working during class time. - sharing ideas, concepts and creative exploration. - sharing conceptual development in progress and information in making with other students. - cooperating in group projects. - analyzing and offering opinions about work in progress. - listening to and being an active participant in critique discussions. ## Bibliography and/or Recommended Texts This bibliography reflects the material used to develop this course. You can access these software tutorials with your current OCAD U username and password. ## Course schedule - **Week 1 (Jan 5):** Introduction. Overview and discussion of class outline, focused on how we will approach Low Poly Game Art, and how we will structure the class. Introduction to the class and class members. Preparation for week 2 individual presentations.. - **Week 2 (Jan 12):** **Presentation & group formation**. All students present their specific learning goals and themes for creation. Based on presentation content, we will form groups to work in the next classes. - **Week 3 (Jan 19):** Game art studio. Pre-production and project set-up. Group & individual tutorials & lecture. - **Week 4 (Jan 26):** **Initial group presentation**. Each team will present their survey on what to work on, why to choose, and how to make. Discussion and critiques will follow. - **Week 5 (Feb 2):** Game art studio. Production. Group & individual tutorials & lecture. - **Week 6 (Feb 9):** Game art studio. Production. Group & individual tutorials & lecture. - **Week 7 (Feb 16):** **Group progress presentation II.** Sharing discoveries to help each other's making. Discussion and critiques. Production. Group & individual tutorials & lecture. - **Study break (Feb 23)** - **Week 8 (Mar 2):** Game art studio. Production & post-production. Group & individual tutorials & lecture. - **Week 9 (Mar 9):** **Final group presentation.** Each team will present their journey through the making process, artist statement, and final results, with a self-analysis. Discussion and critiques will follow. Finalize documentation and submit it. - **Week 10 (Mar 16):** Game art studio. Individual project set-up. Theme can be derived from your group project, or beyond. Applying LOD (level of detail) techniques. - **Week 11 (Mar 23):** **Individual presentation.** Project development, tutorials & lecture. - **Good Friday(Mar 30)** - **Week 12 (Apr 6):** Game art studio. Finalize project. Preparing final presentation & documentation. - **Final presentation & Critique.**
